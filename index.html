Onboard strategy

What’s included, what’s extra, what’s worth it

Your Fantastica booking already gives you enough to have a full holiday without turning the ship into a spending trap. Use the included value hard, then pick only one or two extras that genuinely add to the trip.

Already included

Buffet dining, main dining room meals, shows and entertainment, pool access, gym, open-air sports facilities, children’s entertainment, and Fantastica cabin perks like breakfast delivery and room-service delivery.

Usually extra

Wi‑Fi, bar and café drinks, specialty restaurants, spa, shore excursions, arcade-style paid activities, and professional photos.

Worth booking in advance

Rome transport / excursion, possibly one specialty dinner, and Wi‑Fi for one adult only if truly useful. Everything else can stay selective.

Best family use of MSC World Europa

Use the ship intentionally

MSC World Europa is large enough that wandering it deliberately on day one pays off all week. Prioritise the kids club area, sports facilities, pools, and evening entertainment early so the sea day already feels familiar and exciting.

Do not overspend

Where cruise budgets usually leak

Impulse drink purchases, buying Wi‑Fi for everyone, too many specialty dining bookings, and overloading paid excursions. The strongest value play is included dining + included entertainment + one or two carefully chosen extras.

Port playbook

How to avoid hectic port days

Each port needs a different energy. The trick is not “doing the most”; it is matching the right pace to the right stop.

Genoa

Low-stress, local, family-friendly. Old port and aquarium area are enough.

Rome

Highest logistics day. One zone only, then return early.

Messina

Recovery stop. Easy lunch, wandering and back onboard with energy left.

Valletta & Barcelona

Stylish walking ports. Compact, scenic, rewarding — but still avoid over-packing them.
